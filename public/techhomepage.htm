What is techwear?

The future is matte. Born out of the unlikely crossover between fashion and the outdoors industry, urban techwear (or “techwear”, for short) is an emerging style that blends form with a relentless focus on function. While the athleisure style often straddles the line between aesthetics and utility, techwear chooses to ignore the line altogether: in our technology-enabled future, why couldn’t someone have both?

Brands

    Nike

    Nike, Inc. is an American multinational corporation that is engaged in the design, development, manufacturing, and worldwide marketing and sales of footwear, apparel, equipment, accessories, and services. Nike is a long-standing staple in everyday clothing and functional clothing which makes it a suitable choice for buyers in search of clothing geared towards leisure or a specific purpose.

    RiotDivision

    Geared moreso towards the functionality and pushing the boundaries of its clothing in an acceptable sense. Riot Division is considerably utilitarian influenced in the sense that many of their pieces are focused on modularity and resistance to the elements under certain conditions.

    TheNorthFace

    The North Face, Inc. is an American outdoor product company specializing in outerwear, fleece, coats, shirts, footwear, and equipment such as backpacks, tents, and sleeping bags.Its clothing and equipment lines are catered towards college students, climbers, mountaineers, skiers, snowboarders, hikers, and endurance athletes.The company sponsors professional athletes from the worlds of running, climbing, skiing and snowboarding.

    Acronym

    A very high-end brand based in Berlin. ACRONYM clothing is considered one of the pioneers of techwear clothing due to their overall popularity and influence in terms of pushing techwear towards a reputable position within the fashion industry. ACRONYM clothing can run upwards of a thousand euros.

Influences

Antwon is an avid Youtuber and tech enthusiast who typically posts content on a weekly basis. His in-depth analyses on items are normally composed of their technical attributes, price range, overall worth, availability, personal opinion, and other factors which may sway buyers towards or away from specific pieces.
Errolson Hugh is a Canadian designer and co-founder of his Berlin-based brand ACRONYM. His designs have paved the path for like-minded brands such as Burton, Stoned Island, UNITED ARROWS, and Herno Laminar. He is also on board as the designer of Nike's subsidiary revival ACG line which combines aspects of athleisure and techwear. His pieces can run upwards of a thousand euros and are often sought after within the community for their sleek silohouettes and technical properties.
Jennifer Bin is one of the few female faces of techwear in a seemingly male-dominated region of the clothing industry. She combines picturesque backgrops with her own tech-influenced style in order to convey her unique style.
